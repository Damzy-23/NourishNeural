# Research Proposal: Intelligent Food Management Systems

## Title: "Intelligent Food Management Systems: A Machine Learning Approach to Household Food Waste Reduction Through Predictive Analytics"

## Problem Motivation & Global Context

Food waste represents a significant global challenge, with households responsible for a substantial portion of total waste. According to the Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO, 2021), food waste costs the global economy approximately $1.3 trillion annually, with households contributing significantly to this figure. In the UK specifically, WRAP (2022) reports that 9.5 million tonnes of food are wasted yearly, costing families an average of £700 annually while contributing to environmental degradation through increased CO2 emissions.

Despite widespread consumer awareness of food waste issues, behavioral change remains minimal due to several factors including poor inventory management, inaccurate expiry predictions, and lack of personalized guidance. This represents a critical opportunity for AI intervention at the intersection of environmental sustainability and consumer behavior modification.

## Research Gap & Literature Position

While existing literature demonstrates AI's potential in supply chain optimization (Chen et al., 2021) and waste management at industrial scales, there is insufficient research on household-level AI interventions. Current food management applications show limited effectiveness in waste reduction, indicating significant room for improvement through advanced machine learning techniques.

Furthermore, existing solutions often fail to address the psychological barriers to waste reduction, such as the tendency for consumers to over-purchase perishables due to optimistic consumption planning (Aschemann-Witzel et al., 2019). This research aims to fill this gap by developing AI-powered solutions specifically designed for household food management.

## Research Question & Objectives

**Primary Research Question:** Can AI-powered predictive analytics achieve significant household food waste reduction by systematically addressing the primary waste drivers: poor inventory tracking, inaccurate expiry prediction, and suboptimal purchasing decisions?

**Research Objectives:**
1. Develop machine learning models for food waste prediction using publicly available datasets
2. Create intelligent inventory management systems with predictive capabilities
3. Establish empirical evidence for AI's role in promoting sustainable consumption patterns
4. Evaluate the effectiveness of AI-driven recommendations in reducing household food waste

## Methodology & Innovation

This research will employ a systematic approach to developing and evaluating AI-powered food management systems:

### Dataset Selection
The research will utilize publicly available datasets with appropriate licensing:
- **Food-101 Dataset** (Creative Commons license): For food classification tasks
- **Open Food Facts** (Open Database License): For nutritional and expiry information
- **Household consumption data** from government sources (e.g., UK Office for National Statistics)
- **Food waste datasets** from academic institutions with open access policies

### Technical Approach
The methodology will explore and identify the most suitable machine learning models considering the specific tasks and available infrastructure:

1. **Food Classification:** Implement computer vision techniques for accurate food item identification
2. **Expiry Prediction:** Develop predictive models incorporating environmental factors and historical consumption patterns
3. **Recommendation Systems:** Create personalized recipe and shopping recommendations using collaborative filtering techniques

### Evaluation Framework
- Comparative analysis against existing baseline methods
- Performance metrics including accuracy, precision, and recall
- User experience evaluation through controlled testing scenarios
- Waste reduction measurement using established methodologies

## Expected Outcomes

This research aims to:
- Develop robust AI models for household food management
- Provide empirical evidence for AI's effectiveness in waste reduction
- Create a replicable framework for sustainable AI applications
- Contribute to the advancement of intelligent systems in consumer applications
